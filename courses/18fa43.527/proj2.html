Requirements

  • Render the scene in two different viewports as in the Figure 1.
    • In the left viewport, the (perspective) camera should be located such that the whole scene is viewed as in the figure.
    • You do NOT need to render the view frustum as in the Figure 1.
    • In the right viewport, the scene viewed from the (perspective) camera attached on the bottom of the chopper should be rendered. The scene should change according to the chopper movement.
    • For multiple viewports, refer to this example.
  • Render the landscape.
      Draw a quad-shaped landscape with its size 2x2 on the x-y plane with its center at the origin of the “world coordinates system”. (The “up” direction is the z-axis.) See Figure 2.
      <Figure 2>
      Make the quad texture-mapped with an image you chose. (Be careful with the license.)
  • Render a chopper.
    • The body and the rotor should be rendered as 3D mesh.
      • You have to use vertex attributes for the color.
    • The rotor should keep rotating as project #1.
    • Make the chopper look as fancy as you want.
  • Implement a keyboard controller.
    • Up: Move forward by 0.05
    • Down: Move backward by 0.05
    • Left: Rotate counterclockwise by 10 degree
    • Right: Rotate clockwise by 10 degree
    • W/w: Move upward in the air by 0.05
    • S/s: Move downward in the air by 0.05
