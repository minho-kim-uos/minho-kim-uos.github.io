Symmetric Box-Splines on the An* Lattice
and
Journal of Approximation Theory, 162(9), pp. 1607-1630, Sep. 2010
abstract
Sampling and reconstruction of generic multivariate functions is more efficient on non-Cartesian root lattices, such as the BCC (Body-Centered Cubic) lattice, than on the Cartesian lattice. We introduce a new nxn generator matrix A* enables, in n variables, efficient reconstruction on the non-Cartesian root lattice An* by a symmetric box-spline family Mr*. A2* is the hexagonal lattice and A3* is the BCC lattice. We point out the similarities and differences of Mr* with respect to the popular Cartesian-shifted box-spline family Mr, document the main properties of Mr* and the partition induced by its knot planes and construct, in n variables, the optimal quasi-interpolant of M2*.
